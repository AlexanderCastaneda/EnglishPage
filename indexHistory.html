Norte de Santander is one of thirty-two departments of Colombia, with its capital in Cúcuta.

Due to its natural resources and its geographic location, it’s a commercial and economic axis of the country, in the same way, it’s known as a big rural production, particularly in the agricultural sector.
During the precolombina age, the territory of this department was inhabited by people indigenous Chitareros and by Motilones descendants of the Chibchas. They took refuge in the Caribbean mountain range.

With the arrival of some invasives, how Spaniards, the European first in step ground Norte Santandereano was the Aleman Ambrosio Alfinger. He left the Coro with his troops looking for the gold he arrived at área located on the river bank Magdalena, Where maintained a fight with some people indigenous.
Then Ambrosio Alfinger continued to Giron Satander and traveled from south to north in the Ocaña province but after he was killed by the indigenous people Chimilla nicknamed “Fransisquillo” in the current municipality of Chinacota. Dead Alfinger, Fedro de San Martin took the troup control and with it returned to Coro passing through Cucuta.

In 1541 Hernan Peres de Quezada arrived in Chinacota territory, but had to return in that same year because of indigenous people resistance. Shortly after, Alfonso Perez de Tolosa went out of Tocuyo – Venezuela, and arrived until Salazar de las Palmas passing through Cúcuta, but he also had to return after losing many people in a fight that he lost.
